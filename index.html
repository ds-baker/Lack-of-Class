Strategic Notes — Jordan-Centric System

The Core Principle

Every formation and lineup decision starts with one question: where is Jordan playing tonight? He's the engine of the team — the player who covers the most ground, wins the most duels, and makes the most progressive passes. The system is designed so that Jordan always has a central role with freedom to influence both phases of play.

On a typical 5-player night, the 4-1-2-1-2 Narrow puts Jordan at CM as the connector between defense and attack. Dylan ahead creating, Mike and Charlie finishing, Jake (or AI) behind cleaning up. The narrow shape keeps everyone within Jordan's passing range.

The Dylan–Jordan Flex

The most powerful tactical tool Lack of Class has is the ability to swap Dylan and Jordan's vertical positioning. This gives you two looks from the same formation:

Jordan at CM, Dylan at CAM (Default)

Jordan controls the midfield and feeds Dylan, who threads final balls to Mike/Charlie. Best when you're dominating possession and need Jordan covering ground.

Jordan at CAM, Dylan at CM/CDM (Chasing)

Jordan pushes into the final third as a shadow striker. Dylan drops deep and sprays passes. Best when you need Jordan's quality closer to goal. Use when trailing.

This swap requires no formation change — just a verbal call between the two of you. The opponent sees the same shape but gets a completely different threat.

Formation by Headcount

5 Players — Mike, Charlie, Dylan, Jordan + 1

4-1-2-1-2 Narrow. Two strikers (Mike + Charlie), CAM (Dylan), CM (Jordan), CDM (+1 or AI). Compact, central, deadly. Jordan is the heartbeat.

6 Players — + Jake or Brian

4-1-2-1-2 if Jake joins (he takes CDM, Jordan stays CM). 4-2-3-1 Wide if Brian joins (Brian takes a wing, Dylan can drop into the pivot or Jordan pushes to CAM). Flexible based on who's the 6th.

7-8 Players — Full or Near-Full

4-2-3-1 Wide. Jake + Jordan in the double pivot (Jordan B2B, Jake holds). Dylan at CAM. Mike LW, Brian RW, Charlie ST. Max/Adam fill remaining spots.

Archetype Synergy

The Maestro archetype is ideal for Jordan. Pinged+ means his long switches to Mike on the left and Brian/Charlie on the right are consistently accurate. Tiki Taka+ means he retains the ball under pressure and plays quick one-twos with Dylan. Long Ball+ gives a counter-attacking outlet when the team wins possession deep.

The Jake (Recycler) + Jordan (Maestro) partnership is the dream double pivot: one wins the ball, the other moves it. Jake's Intercept+ feeds directly into Jordan's Pinged+ — turnover to progressive pass in one touch. Add Dylan's Creator with Incisive Pass, and the central triangle of Jake → Jordan → Dylan is incredibly hard to deal with.

Player Roles

Jordan (CM/CDM) → Box-to-Box or Deep-Lying Playmaker. Never "Holding" — that's Jake's job. Jordan needs license to roam and drive forward.

Dylan (CAM) → Playmaker to link play; Shadow Striker to get in the box. At CDM: Deep-Lying Playmaker.

Mike (LW) → Inside Forward. Always. Cut inside, shoot.

Charlie (ST) → Target Man (Target archetype) or Advanced Forward (Finisher). The focal point.

Jake (CDM) → Holding. Always. The anchor.

Tactical Settings

Defensive depth: 60 — compresses space so Jordan doesn't cover enormous distances between lines, but isn't reckless.

Build-up: Balanced — lets the team play what's in front of them. Switch to Short Passing to slow things down and let Jordan orchestrate; Counter if absorbing pressure and springing Mike/Brian on the break.

Width: Narrow in the 4-1-2-1-2 (AI fullbacks provide width). Default in the 4-2-3-1 to stretch the pitch for Jordan's switches.

AI Facility Investment

With 5-6 humans most nights, AI plays GK, 2 CBs, 2 FBs, and sometimes CDM. Prioritize Defender facilities first, then Goalkeeper, then Midfield. A strong AI back line lets Jordan push forward without worrying about what's behind him. At max reputation (2.5M budget), aim for roughly 40/30/20/10 across Defenders/GK/Midfield/Attackers.
